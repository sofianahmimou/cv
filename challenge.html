I am a Professor at Cornell University. My research focuses on issues at the interface of network and information, with an emphasis in social and information networks and underpin web and other online media.

My work has been supported by NSF Career Award, an ONR Young Investigator Award, a MacArthur Foundation Fellowship, a Simon Investigator Award, a Sloan Investigator Fellowship, and grants from Facebook, Google, Yahoo!, the ARO, and the NSF. I am a member of the National Academy of Science, the National Academy of Engineering, and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

Books and Teaching

  • D. Easley, J. Kleinberg. Networks, Crowds, Markets: Reasoning About A Highly Connected World, Cambridge University Press, 2010.
    • This book is based on an interdisciplinary course that we teach entitled Network. The book, like the course, is designed at the introductory undergraduate level with no formal prerequisite. To support deeper exploration, most of chapters are supplemented with optional advanced sections.
  • J. Kleinberg, E. Tardos. Algorithm Design, Addison Wesley, 2005.
    • This book is based on the undergraduate algorithm course we both teach. We also use the more advanced part for our graduate algorithm course.
  • An online course on edX entitled Network, Crowds, Markets, with David Easley and Eva Tardos

    • The Structure of Information Networks (CS 6850)
    • Algorithm (CS 6850)
